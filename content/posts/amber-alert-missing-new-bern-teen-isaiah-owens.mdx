The New Bern Police Department has issued an urgent Amber Alert for 15-year-old Isaiah Kavon Owens, a teenager with autism and a speech delay, who was reported missing on Saturday morning. Authorities believe Owens is with 43-year-old Kenneth Lee Brantley and may be traveling in a black 2012 GMC Yukon with South Carolina license plates. This alert mobilizes public assistance, highlighting the critical need for community vigilance in the rapid and safe recovery of vulnerable individuals.

</Callout>

## What happened
On Saturday at 8:28 a.m., the New Bern Police Department initiated an Amber Alert for Isaiah Kavon Owens. Isaiah is described as a 15-year-old Black male, approximately 5 feet 4 inches tall and weighing 100 pounds, with black hair and brown eyes. He was last observed wearing a white hoodie, black pants, and red and blue sneakers. Due to his autism and speech delay, Isaiah is considered particularly vulnerable.

Authorities suspect Isaiah is in the company of Kenneth Lee Brantley, a 43-year-old Black male, 5 feet 2 inches tall, weighing 162 pounds, with black hair and brown eyes. Brantley was last seen at the Palace Motel, Room 242, located at 1901 Martin Luther King Jr. Blvd, wearing a white t-shirt with a colorful design and gray pants. The pair is believed to be traveling in a black 2012 GMC Yukon bearing South Carolina license plate XFH-752.

## Why it matters
The issuance of an Amber Alert signifies a high-priority search for a missing child believed to be in danger. In Isaiah's case, his autism and speech delay heighten concerns for his safety and well-being, making immediate public awareness and response paramount. The system leverages broad public communication channels to disseminate critical information quickly, significantly increasing the chances of a safe recovery by turning every citizen into a potential lookout. The urgency is amplified when a vulnerable individual is believed to be with an adult male, underscoring the potential risks involved.

## How to think about it
When an Amber Alert is issued, it's crucial to understand your role as a member of the public. The primary goal is to provide accurate information to law enforcement without directly intervening. If you see the described individuals or vehicle, do not approach them. Instead, immediately call 911 or the New Bern Police Department at (252) 633-2020. Pay close attention to details such as location, direction of travel, and any distinguishing features of the vehicle or individuals. Your prompt and precise reporting can make a critical difference in the outcome of the search.

<Callout type="warning">
  Do NOT attempt to approach or apprehend the individuals described in an Amber Alert. Your safety is paramount, and direct intervention could escalate a dangerous situation. Always contact law enforcement immediately with any information.
</Callout>
