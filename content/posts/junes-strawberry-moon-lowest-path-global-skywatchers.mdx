Its dramatic rise and low trajectory provided unique photographic opportunities and a memorable experience for countless skywatchers, highlighting the dynamic interplay between Earth's orbit and lunar phases.

## What happened

The full moon in June, dubbed the Strawberry Moon, captivated stargazers worldwide with its striking appearance. For observers in the Northern Hemisphere, this particular full moon was the lowest-hanging of the entire year. This phenomenon occurs because a full moon always positions itself opposite the sun in the sky. Given that the summer solstice, which occurred on June 21, saw the sun follow its highest annual path, the full moon consequently took the inverse route, skimming very low across the southern horizon.

Rising in the southeast and setting in the southwest, the Strawberry Moon remained close to the horizon throughout the night. This low altitude made it an exceptional subject for photographers and enthusiasts, who captured its beauty against various iconic backdrops. Images from locations such as the Port of Molfetta in Italy, the Manhattan skyline in New York City, San Francisco Bay, and ancient sites like the Temple of Poseidon in Greece showcased the moon's vibrant orange hues and dramatic presence.

## How to think about it

When observing or anticipating celestial events like the Strawberry Moon, it's helpful to understand the basic mechanics driving them. The full moon's position relative to the sun and Earth's axial tilt are key factors. For low-hanging moons, consider your local horizon and any potential obstructions like buildings or trees. Planning your viewing location in advance, ideally with an unobstructed view towards the southeast for moonrise and southwest for moonset, can significantly enhance your experience. Remember that the moon's apparent size and color can be influenced by atmospheric conditions, with a lower moon often appearing larger and more orange due to atmospheric scattering. Embrace these natural variations as part of the unique beauty of each lunar event.
