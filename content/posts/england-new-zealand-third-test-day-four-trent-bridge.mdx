## What happened
On day four at Trent Bridge, New Zealand's Daryl Mitchell and Tom Blundell established a critical partnership, frustrating England's attempts to secure breakthroughs. Blundell, after a 129-run stand with Rachin Ravindra, joined Mitchell to continue their impressive Test cricket record, having previously amassed 1,134 runs together since 2021, including four century partnerships and two fifty-run stands. Their combined resilience has made them New Zealand's second-most successful batting pair in Tests over this period.

England's bowlers, including Jofra Archer and captain Ben Stokes, exerted considerable pressure, but struggled to dislodge the well-set batsmen. Archer delivered tight overs, occasionally beating the outside edge of Mitchell's bat, while Stokes bowled wide of the crease, angling into Mitchell. Despite appeals, such as a "desperation" plea from Stokes for an LBW against Mitchell that was deemed too high, England found wickets elusive, with the ball sometimes eluding fielders for boundaries or byes, including four byes from a pea-roller delivery from Stokes.
