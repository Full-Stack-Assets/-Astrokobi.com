Venezuela's northern coast has been ravaged by a catastrophic sequence of twin earthquakes, measuring magnitudes 7.2 and 7.5, triggering a desperate international rescue effort. As the death toll surpasses 1,400 and tens of thousands remain unaccounted for, search-and-rescue teams from around the globe are working tirelessly amidst the rubble. The critical 72-hour survival window for those trapped under collapsed structures has now passed, intensifying the urgency and shifting focus toward recovery while still holding onto faint hopes for more miraculous rescues.

## What happened
On Wednesday, a pair of powerful earthquakes, registering magnitudes 7.2 and 7.5, struck Venezuela's northern coast in quick succession, causing widespread devastation. The worst-hit area is La Guaira state, where entire apartment blocks, hotels, and public housing buildings collapsed. The official death toll has climbed to 1,430 as of late Saturday, with over 3,000 injured and a similar number displaced into shelters.

Despite the grim statistics, rescue operations have yielded some successes. Over the weekend, 33 individuals were pulled alive from the debris, including an infant rescued by U.S. teams and two 11-year-old children found by Colombian and Mexican crews. However, the number of missing remains highly uncertain, with estimates ranging from just under 50,000 to over 68,900, reflecting the chaos and communication failures in the aftermath. Hundreds of aftershocks continue to complicate rescue efforts and keep survivors out of damaged homes.

As the critical 72-hour period for finding survivors under rubble passed Saturday evening, the odds of further live rescues diminish significantly, as noted by experts like Swiss rescue-team leader Sebastian Eugster. In response to the communication challenges, Starlink Mobile has stepped in, providing free connectivity to customers in the La Guaira region, enabling families and communities to stay connected via SMS even when terrestrial networks are down. Pope Leo also expressed solidarity with the victims and their families, offering prayers and closeness.

## Why it matters
The scale of the devastation in Venezuela represents a profound humanitarian crisis, placing immense strain on local resources and requiring significant international aid. The sheer number of missing persons, even with varying estimates, underscores the widespread impact on families and communities, many of whom face an agonizing wait for news of their loved ones. The collapse of numerous residential and public buildings highlights vulnerabilities in infrastructure and the urgent need for resilient construction practices in earthquake-prone regions.

This disaster also demonstrates the critical importance of rapid, coordinated international response in the immediate aftermath of such events. The successful rescues, though few compared to the missing, offer a glimmer of hope and validate the efforts of global humanitarian teams. Furthermore, the deployment of satellite communication services like Starlink underscores how technology can play a vital role in disaster relief, helping to bridge communication gaps when traditional networks fail, which is crucial for both rescue coordination and connecting affected populations.
