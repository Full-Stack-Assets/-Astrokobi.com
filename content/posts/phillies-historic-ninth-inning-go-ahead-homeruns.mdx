The Philadelphia Phillies have etched their name into Major League Baseball history, achieving a feat never before accomplished. In a remarkable display of late-game heroics, the team hit a go-ahead home run in the ninth inning of three consecutive games against the Washington Nationals. This unprecedented streak, culminating in Bryce Harper's dramatic two-run shot, highlights the Phillies' incredible resilience and clutch performance, captivating fans and rewriting the record books.

## What happened
The historic run began with two prior games before Thursday night's dramatic 10-5 comeback victory over the Washington Nationals. In the latest installment, the Phillies found themselves trailing 5-0 after five innings. The comeback was ignited in the sixth inning by Brandon Marsh's two-run home run, cutting the deficit to 5-2. The team then capitalized on four walks in the seventh inning to score three runs, tying the game at 5-5.

The decisive moment arrived in the top of the ninth inning when former Nationals star Bryce Harper stepped to the plate. With the score tied, Harper launched a two-run opposite-field home run, his 18th of the season, off Nationals reliever Gus Varland. This monumental blast broke the tie and secured the Phillies' third straight ninth-inning go-ahead homer, a feat confirmed by the Elias Sports Bureau as a Major League first. Marsh continued his hot streak throughout the series, finishing 9-for-17 with three homers and six RBIs.
